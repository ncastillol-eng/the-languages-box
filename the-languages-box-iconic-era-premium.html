Mission 1

Create Your Iconic Character

Students build an original character they will use across all missions. This creates continuity and makes speaking feel safer and more playful.

Speaking structure

My character seems..., but actually...
People think my character is..., however...
Deep down, my character feels...
By the end, my character learns that...

Upgrade your answer

Basic: She is popular.

Better: She seems popular, but she feels lonely.

Advanced: Although she is admired by everyone, her public image has become a mask that hides her insecurity.

Common mistakes

Don’t say: People thinks. Say: People think.

Don’t say: She has 18 years. Say: She is 18 years old.

Popularity, Cliques & Social Survival

Talk about reputation, belonging, gossip and power dynamics.

  • Language: contrast, reasons, examples

Vocabulary Deck

High-value words for better speaking

Each word includes meaning, Spanish support, pronunciation focus and an example students can adapt.

misunderstood
Spanish: incomprendido/a

When people do not understand who you really are.

She seems cold, but she is misunderstood.
people-pleaser
Spanish: persona que busca complacer a todos

Someone who tries to make everyone happy, even at their own cost.

He is tired of being a people-pleaser.
comeback
Spanish: respuesta ingeniosa / regreso fuerte

A smart reply or a powerful return after a difficult moment.

Her comeback left everyone speechless.
vulnerable
Pronunciation: practice /v/, not /b/

Open about feelings, fears or weaknesses.

Being vulnerable is not weakness.
deal-breaker
Spanish: algo inaceptable

A problem that makes a relationship or situation impossible to continue.

Dishonesty is a deal-breaker for me.
self-worth
Spanish: valor propio

The sense that you are valuable, regardless of approval or popularity.

Your self-worth should not depend on popularity.
